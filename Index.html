Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Born:- 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram

Died:- 27 July 2015 in Shillong

The First Missile Man Of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam is known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work in developing India's missile and nuclear weapons programs. APJ Abdul Kalam who launched the first Satellite Launch Vehicle, SLV-3 putting the Rohini Satellite in orbit. The IGMDP project began on July 26, 1983 at the Defence Research and Development Laboratory (DRDL) in Hyderabad.


Contributions to space and missile programs

Kalam was a Project Director at ISRO, where he helped develop India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle. He also played a key role in the development of the AGNI and PRITHVI missiles, and was involved in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.


Other achievements

Kalam was also an aeronautical engineer, and was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. He was also the recipient of honorary doctorates from 30 universities and institutions.


President of India

Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the "People's President".

Birth anniversary

Kalam's birth anniversary, October 15, is celebrated as World Students' Day to honor his dedication to education.
